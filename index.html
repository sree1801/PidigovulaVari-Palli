Yadava's Hierarchy

Pidigovulavaripalli is a small village located in the KhambhamvariPalli Post, Sadum Mandal, Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, India.

Pidigovulavaripalli is a charming rural village in India, comprising 75 houses. Deeply rooted in Indian traditions passed down by their ancestors, the villagers maintain harmonious and friendly relations with one another. The entire population belongs to the Yadava dynasty, followers of Lord Krishna, and they adhere to age-old customs with great reverence.

Elders

The elders in Pidigovulavaripalli are cherished members of the community. During the day, they engage in their household chores, but as the sun sets, they gather outside their homes on what is known locally as "arugu" in Telugu. These evening meetings are filled with joyful conversations, laughter, and storytelling, which bring smiles to their faces. Supper time is a cherished ritual, where the whole family comes together to share meals. This practice fosters friendly and supportive discussions, allowing everyone to stay connected and informed about each other's lives, including the education and progress of the children.

Parents

Parents in the village are the custodians of tradition, having learned the ways of life from the elders. They diligently pass on these customs, rules, and values to their children. Responsible for the bulk of the household work, parents ensure the well-being and smooth functioning of their families. They are the backbone of the village, balancing their roles as caretakers, educators, and providers.
